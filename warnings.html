About PKI Warnings

PKI is the system encrypted web sites rely on to assert their trustworthyness. It is intended to provide an assurance that you don't end up looking at an imposter when you type "https://justhide.me" into your browser. It does this through a network of Certificate Authorities whom your web browser has trusted to say "this web site is really what it says it is." If you visit an encrypted web site that does not have the seal of approval (in the form of a cryptographic digital signature) from an authority your browser trusts, you get a warning message.

In the case of a JustHideMe ephemeral VPN, the certificate authority we use is also ephemeral. It is created inside the VPN's Docker container when the container. It is therefore impossible for your browser to know in advance that it is okay to trust this CA's authority to approve anything. That is why, when you visit a JustHideMe client config download site, you get a warning message.

While you should never make a habit of ignoring warning messages, it is possible to easily verify that it is safe to proceed inspite of the warning on a JustHideMe download link.

A block of text like this appears beneath the download link

CONFIRM BEFORE IGNORING ANY SECURITY WARNINGS:
	READ THIS:	https://justhideme.github.io/warnings.html
	Cert Name:	web.EH5XrAA2.justhide.me
	Cert SHA1:	AA:D3:7F:1B:77:13:13:3B:5E:A5:4D:62:25:8F:42:FB:26:28:D7:8F
	Issued by:	ca.EH5XrAA2.justhide.me
	Issuer SHA1:	37:B5:5A:10:7B:AF:60:8B:D0:B0:37:9C:78:B1:0A:47:4E:79:42:AD
